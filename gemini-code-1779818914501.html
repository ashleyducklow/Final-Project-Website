Official Performance Genres

At the UDA National Championship, collegiate teams can showcase routines across four primary competitive dance styles. Each style values distinct athletic elements, pacing, and visual targets.

Jazz Style

Focuses on technical execution, control, and stylistic expression. Teams incorporate continuous complex turn sequences, elite leaps, synchronization, and profound emotional execution or musicality.

Pom Style

Highly energetic style characterized by the mandatory use of pom-poms. Focuses heavily on precise, clean, visual arm work, rapid formations transitions, and dynamic team synchronization.

Hip Hop

Emphasizes street dance choreography, rhythm, execution, and group identity. Teams integrate distinct isolations, musical textures, floor tricks, and a commanding performance presence.

Did you know? In addition to these three core fields, teams can also enter the Game Day Division, which evaluates how effectively a dance team can lead a university stadium crowd alongside their school pep-bands and cheer squads!

The Judging Framework

Panels of highly qualified industry professionals analyze performance across two core scoring tracks:

  • Execution & Technical Skill: Measures the physical synchronization, turn alignment, jump extension, placement consistency, and stamina of the dance roster.
  • Choreography & Presentation: Measures musicality, transition creativity, dynamic visuals, and overall visual effect.

The Road to Orlando

Qualifying for and performing at the UDA National Championships at the ESPN Wide World of Sports Complex is a demanding multi-month commitment.

1. Summer Camp Qualification

The journey begins in July or August. Teams must attend a mandatory UDA regional camp, master intensive curriculum, and earn a "Superior Trophy" designation or specific bids to qualify officially for nationals.

2. Autumn Choreography

Choreographers are secured by late summer or early fall. Routines are completely structured and polished over hundreds of hours through the autumn academic calendar alongside game-day obligations.

3. The Elimination Track

Once in Orlando, teams do not just perform once. They face intense multi-day schedules climbing from Preliminaries to Semi-Finals, before a highly selective fraction of teams secure a spot on the Finals floor.

About the Championship

Every year in mid-January, hundreds of elite university dance teams travel to the Walt Disney World Resort in Orlando, Florida. They compete head-to-head for the legendary white jackets and National Title trophies. This platform celebrates the intersection of elite athleticism, artistry, and university spirit.
